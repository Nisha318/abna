Letter From the President

Greetings,

As President of Acadiana Black Nurses Association (ABNA) Chapter of the National Black Nurses Association, I welcome each of you to our website. I am enthusiastic about continuing the efforts of the ABNA to enhance the health of vulnerable and minority populations in the Acadiana area.

As President of ABNA, I feel fortunate to be an active member of one of the most unique volunteer organizations serving on an international level. As a retired nurse clinician/educator, researcher, and consultant, I consider it both an honor and a blessing to volunteer a portion of my knowledge, skills, talents, and time that 45 years of nursing has afforded me in giving back to society.

”To whom much is given, much will be required.”  If one is blessed with talents, skills, knowledge, time … what hinders one from using a portion of those assets to benefit others? Throughout the year, ABNA positions itself strategically to meet the mission of the National Black Nurses Association (NBNA) "to represent a forum for black nurses to advocate and implement strategies to ensure access to the highest quality of healthcare for persons of color."

ABNA works collaboratively with community health organizations to address health disparities among vulnerable and minority populations through community health walks, health screening/promotion events, and community clinics/camps. Our organization is actively engaged in efforts to assist potential nurses to access the academic arena and progress to RN/LPN licensure. ABNA has set up two awards programs:

  • Dr. Enrica Kinchen Singleton (EKS) scholarship - for students graduating from Associates or BSN programs demonstrating the qualities of scholarship, service, and professionalism during their academic tensure in nursing; and
  • Community Volunteer - for community members who whose community health volunteer efforts clearly align with those of NBNA disparity initiatives.

    • If you are not already a member of and would like to learn more about ABNA, feel free to contact any member for details.

    Respectfully,

    Dr. Nellie Prudhomme, RN-BC, DNS
    President, Acadiana Black Nurses Association
